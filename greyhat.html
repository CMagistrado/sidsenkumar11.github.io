Meeting 1 Problems

This first week's problem set will be about cryptography. Cryptography is the art of code making and cryptanalysis is the art of code breaking. I'll be dividing the problems up into 2 categories to give more people a chance to learn during these meetings. You can also view my slides from last semester here.

Generally, crypto problems will go something like this: I will give you a text and I'd like for you to decrypt this text. If you think you've got the plaintext (readable text) or have any questions, feel free to ask me and I'll do my best to help you!

Intermediate

There is another encryption scheme called the "One Time Pad". This is quite famous because it is known to be one of the only "perfect" encryptions - that is, when implemented correctly, it has been mathematically proven to be impossible to break.

The way the one time pad works is as follows:

1) Generate a random key at least as long as the plaintext.
2) XOR the plaintext with the key.

It is definitely a very simple algorithm, and can lead to very secure encryption, but when not implemented correctly can lead to vulnerability.
